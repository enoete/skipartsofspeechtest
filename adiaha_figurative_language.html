The Figurative Language Devices 🌈

Everything from your notebook — studied, summarised, and served with extra sauce 💅

🔭

Simile

A comparison between two things using the words LIKE, AS, or THAN. It's a weaker comparison than a metaphor — only SOME things are being compared.

💀 "Simile is like the junior version of metaphor ngl"
Examples from your notes
"He is like a rocket on his bike."
"The car was as fast as the wind."
"Harry ran faster than the wind."
"The fresh apple was crispy like a new dollar bill."
"The swing rocked like a baby learning how to walk."
💫

Metaphor

A direct comparison between two things WITHOUT using like, as, or than. It says one thing IS something else. Compares ALL elements/features — stronger than a simile!

🔥 "Metaphors just say it straight. No 'like' or 'as' needed — main character behaviour."
Examples from your notes
"Her eyes are twinkling stars in the night sky."
"Coffee is life as it awakens me."
"The stage was the canvas upon which the play was painted."
"Fruit is fuel for a healthy body."
"Music is medicine for the soul."
"The clock is the keeper of time."
🧍

Personification

Giving human attributes to inanimate (non-living) objects. It brings non-living things alive by describing them doing things a human would do. Helps you imagine things more clearly!

😭 "Personification is when objects start acting all human and dramatic fr"
Examples from your notes
"The alarm screamed as it woke me." (alarm = object; screamed = human action)
"The boat slept peacefully on the water."
"The fire roared to life."
"The glasses danced on the shelf during the earthquake."
"The rusty door hinge screamed every time the door opened."
"The water called invitingly to the hot children."
"The train screamed as it made its way to us."
📢

Hyperbole

A gross exaggeration used to emphasise a point. Hyperboles CANNOT literally happen — they are impossible in real life!

🗣️ "Hyperboles are when you're being EXTRA on purpose. Absolutely no cap intended."
Examples from your notes
"I am so hungry I could eat a horse." (you literally cannot eat an entire horse 😭)
"Mrs. Terry gave us a mountain of homework."
"The phone rang a million times today."
"I need as many flowers as I kill when I walk by."
"Mary sweats buckets after her race."
"The car was a jet when it passed us."
🔤

Alliteration

The repetition of the same sound three or more times in close succession. Found almost always in poetry. Two types: Assonance (vowel sounds) and Consonance (consonant sounds).

✨ "Alliteration when the sentence says the same sound on repeat like a banger hook"
Assonance (vowel sound repetition)
"Atom Ant and his amazing abilities" → repetition of 'ah' sound
"How now brown cow?" → repetition of 'ow' sound
Consonance (consonant sound repetition)
"Peter Piper picked a peck of pickled peppers" → repetition of 'p' and 'ck' sounds
"Betty Boughter bought a bit of bitter butter" → 'b' and 't' sounds
🔊

Onomatopoeia

The written expression of a sound on paper. It mimics the sound in real life — when you read it, you can HEAR it!

💥 "Onomatopoeia is when words literally sound like the thing they describe. SLAY."
Examples from your notes
"The air is filled with the BUZZING of the bees."
"CLICKY, CLOCKY, CLICKY CLOCK went her heels across the wooden floor."
Other examples: BANG, CRASH, SIZZLE, HISS, BOOM, SPLASH
🗣️

Idiom

A common phrase or expression that CANNOT be understood from the literal definitions of the original words. We call these idiomatic expressions!

🤯 "Idioms are like secret codes — the words say one thing but mean something totally different."
Examples from your notes
"Break a leg" = Good luck in a performance
"Piece of cake" = It's really easy
"Under the weather" = Feeling ill / sick
"Bite the bullet" = Force yourself to do something difficult
😂

Pun

A play on words using homophones or homonyms. Usually intended to be humorous, but there are puns that are serious in nature too!

💀 "Puns are the dad jokes of figurative language. Cringe AND genius at the same time."
Examples from your notes
"I can't dance, my soul is too heavy." → SOUL (emotional) vs SOLE (bottom of foot)
"The store gave out batteries free of charge." → CHARGE (cost) vs CHARGE (battery power)
More puns from Page 1 of your notebook!
"Why did the bicycle fall over? Because it was TWO-TIRED." → too tired / two tyres 😭
"What do you call a bear with no teeth? A GUMMY bear." 🐻
"I used to be afraid of hurdles but I GOT OVER IT." 🏃
"What do you call an alligator in a vest? An IN-VEST-igator." 🐊
"Why can't you trust stairs? They're always UP TO SOMETHING." 😂
🔁

Oxymoron

Places two contradicting or opposing words together to create a unique, thought-provoking phrase.

🤔 "Oxymorons be out here making no sense but also making perfect sense. The paradox era."
Examples from your notes
Jumbo shrimp • Deafening silence • Bittersweet • Act natural
Original copy • Awfully good • Seriously funny • Sound of silence
📏

Literal Language

When a phrase or word uses absolutely NO figurative devices — it means exactly what it says. No hidden meaning, no comparison, no exaggeration.

😐 "Literal language is just... saying what you mean. That's it. No sauce added."
Examples from your notes
"We had eggs and bacon for breakfast." (exactly what happened)
"It is raining a lot." (just the facts, no drama)

🔭 vs 💫 Simile vs Metaphor — Know the Difference!

FeatureSimileMetaphor
Uses like/as/than?✅ YES❌ NO
Says one thing IS another?❌ NO✅ YES
Strength of comparisonWeaker (only some things)Stronger (all features)
Example"He is LIKE a rocket""He IS a rocket"

🧠 Memory Hacks That Actually Slap

  • Simile = uses Similar words (like, as, than) — S for Similar!
  • Hyperbole = CANNOT literally happen. If it's possible in real life → NOT a hyperbole.
  • Personification = object doing something a HUMAN does (running, crying, screaming, sleeping)
  • Alliteration = same SOUND repeated 3+ times — Assonance (vowels), Consonance (consonants)
  • Onomatopoeia = the word SOUNDS like what it describes (BUZZ, HISS, CLANG)
  • Idiom = you CANNOT understand it from the literal words alone
  • Oxymoron = TWO opposite/contradicting words side by side (jumbo shrimp 😭)
  • Pun = plays on words with homophones (same sound, different meaning) or homonyms (same word, different meaning)
  • Literal = NO figurative devices at all. Just the plain truth.
